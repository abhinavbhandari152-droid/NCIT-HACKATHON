GharSathi – Hackathon Pitch


1. Problem Statement

"How many of us have searched for a room or flat in Kathmandu by walking around and reading posters on electric poles?"

"Even after finding a place, getting a trustworthy plumber, electrician, or cleaner can be difficult—especially during emergencies."

"GharSathi solves these problems by bringing property listings, verified home services, and community engagement into one platform built specifically for the Kathmandu Valley."

2. Our Solution

"GharSathi is more than a housing portal—it's a complete settling-in platform."

"Users can discover rental properties, view them on an interactive map, and instantly find nearby service providers."

"We also include community events and local activities, helping newcomers connect with their neighborhood and build a social network."

3. Technical Implementation

"We built the entire prototype using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript without heavy frameworks."

"We integrated Leaflet.js to provide interactive maps for property listings and service providers."

"We implemented client-side state management using localStorage, allowing users to save properties, bookings, and event registrations even after closing the browser."

"For demonstration purposes, we simulated backend requests and network delays to showcase how the platform will function once connected to a Node.js or Firebase backend."

4. UI/UX Design

"Our goal was simplicity, accessibility, and responsiveness."

"We included dynamic listing counters, dashboard empty states, and toast notifications to provide constant user feedback."

"The platform is fully responsive and optimized for mobile devices, which represent the majority of our target users."

5. Impact

"Kathmandu's rental and home-service ecosystem is fragmented."

"GharSathi creates a single digital ecosystem where people can find homes, access trusted services, and become part of their local community."

"This saves time, reduces uncertainty, and improves the overall experience of moving and living in the city."

6. Q&A Preparation

Q: Why didn't you use React or Next.js?

A: "For a 24–48 hour hackathon, our priority was delivering a stable, fully functional prototype. Vanilla JavaScript allowed us to develop faster, avoid build complexity, and ensure excellent performance on low-end mobile devices."

Q: How will you monetize?

A: "We plan to use a freemium model for property listings and generate revenue through lead-generation commissions from service providers who receive bookings through the platform."

Q: Where does the data come from?

A: "Currently, we use mock data to demonstrate the user experience and application flow. Our next step is integrating a Firebase or Node.js backend with real-world listings and service-provider data."

Q: What makes GharSathi different?

A: "Most platforms focus only on housing or only on services. GharSathi combines housing, home services, mapping, and community engagement into one unified platform."

Final Closing Line:

"GharSathi isn't just helping people find a house. We're helping them find a home, trusted services, and a community—all in one place."
